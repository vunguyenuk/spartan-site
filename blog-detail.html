Everyone gets faster writes: We turned off FPW’s in Spartan

We’ve managed to give customers up to 5x performance increase on write-heavy workloads by disabling full-page writes, a Postgres durability safety feature that is made redundant by Spartan’s own storage engine.

David Wein, Vlad Lazar
May 07, 2026
Everyone gets faster writes: disabling FPW on Spartan
✎ Note

This is a cross-post of an engineering blog that was originally published on Databricks. Spartan and Databricks Lakebase both run on the same technology, and this engineering optimization benefits customers of both.

We’ve managed to give customers up to a 5x performance increase on write-heavy workloads by disabling full page writes (FPW), a Postgres durability safety feature that we made redundant with Spartan’s own storage engine.

The hidden cost of traditional Postgres durability

Full page writes exist to protect against torn pages — the situation where a crash happens mid-write and only part of an 8 KB page reaches disk. To guard against this, Postgres writes the entire page to the write-ahead log the first time it’s touched after a checkpoint.

That safety comes at a price. On write-heavy workloads, FPW can multiply the amount of WAL generated several times over, saturating disk bandwidth and inflating replication lag.

  • WAL amplification: a single row update can emit an 8 KB page image.
  • Checkpoint pressure: tighter checkpoints trade recovery time for more FPW.
  • Replication lag: more WAL means more bytes to ship to every replica.

Spartan storage eliminates the risk of torn pages

Spartan’s storage engine writes pages atomically at the block layer. Because a page can never be torn, the guarantee that FPW provides is already satisfied lower in the stack — which means we can turn it off with no loss of durability.

Image generation pushdown to distributed storage

Rather than reconstruct full-page images in the database tier, Spartan pushes that responsibility down to distributed storage, keeping the WAL lean and the write path short.

FPW’s seamlessly turned off for all databases

For eligible clusters the change rolls out transparently — no downtime, no application changes, and full durability preserved by the storage layer.

What’s Next

We’re pairing this with adaptive checkpoint tuning so write-heavy teams get the gains automatically — no configuration required.
